We’ve seen way too many talented creatives quit because they couldn’t make it work financially. No doubt, the financial challenges of pursuing a creative or artistic career are daunting, but we felt there wasn’t enough discussion around how to make it work. So, we connected with artists and creatives who’ve been able to earn a full time living from their creative work and asked them to share their stories with our readers.

## Just John![Meet Just John](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779231769785-personal_1779231769717_1779231769717_just_john_e0a9cbcf-061f-4795-b0ed-36757731a8e0.jpeg)

I knew the moment I picked up a microphone for the first time at 16 that creativity was what I wanted to dedicate my life to. My ex invited me to a church open mic, and even though I had always written poetry privately, that experience pushed me to finally share my voice. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-just-john-2)

## Clumsy Hands![Meet Clumsy Hands](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779170819314-personal_1779170819005_1779170819005_clumsy_hands_clumsyhands2.jpg)

I feel like this is a mythical thing all artists are striving towards. So many big artists I know are working in coffee shops, part-time or are supported by family and partners. I think we need to all take the pressure off paying our bills with our art in a world that’s on fire. One month I will earn $17k, the next three months nothing. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-clumsy-hands)

## Aiden McDonald![Meet Aiden](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778879243482-personal_1778879243324_1778879243324_aiden_mcdonald_capture-one-042326-0003.jpeg)

I was given the opportunity to work full-time fairly early on in my creative career. I made the decision to pursue videography as a career in about 2017. At that point I was still in high school and I had no idea about anything related to running a business. I just knew that I wanted to do it. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-aiden)

## Aaron Lerer![Meet Aaron Lerer](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778873582727-personal_1778873582639_1778873582639_aaron_lerer_20260515_152323.jpeg)

It didn’t happen overnight, and I wouldn’t say I’m fully ‘there’ yet, but I’m earning a living entirely from my creative work, and that’s something I couldn’t say three years ago. I studied music at IMEP in Paris and Berklee College of Music in Boston. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-aaron-lerer)

## Jonathan Marshall![Meet Jonathan Marshall](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779220453890-personal_1779220453718_1779220453718_jonathan_marshall_jonnytypes-profile.png)

Starting a typewriter repair shop was always my retirement plan. I’ve been a writer my whole life, always interested in the different ways we communicate with one another. When I bought my first typewriter and sat down to type up a story, I was disappointed when it didn’t work. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-jonathan-marshall)

## Harvey Dent![Meet Harvey  Dent](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778863563993-personal_1778863563854_1778863563854_harvey_dent_dj-harvey-dent-impulse-revival.jpg)

This is my third year earning a full time living from DJing and clothing sales. It is something unexpected but I am truly grateful to be able to make a living off of what I create independently. I started a t shirt line in 2016 from a viral moment. I have been a DJ for 24 years. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-harvey-dent)

## Jeral Tidwell![Meet Jeral Tidwell](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779134572101-personal_1779134571843_1779134571843_jeral_tidwell_tidwells-tongues-out-large.jpg)

I’ve been making art professionally, full time since 1988. I actually started a few years earlier while still in high school but I don’t count that part of my professional career. Starting at 15 years old, I would have kids bring t-shirts and $5 to school, I would take them home and airbrush some terrible art on them, I was still learning how to airbrush. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-jeral-tidwell)

## Jorge torrealba![Meet Jorge Torrealba](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779161941735-personal_1779161941542_1779161941542_jorge_torrealba_img_2431.jpeg)

Yes, but it has been a long evolution. Even before moving to the United States, I was already working professionally as an artist and caricaturist in Venezuela. When I arrived in the U.S. almost ten years ago, the challenge wasn’t starting art from zero ,it was rebuilding my career, adapting to a new culture, and finding new ways to connect with people through my work. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-jorge-torrealba)

## Hunter Phoenix![Meet Hunter Phoenix](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779044495878-personal_1779044495599_1779044495599_hunter_phoenix_1-hunterphoenix.jpg)

For a lot of actors, the dream is fame and fortune, mine started out a little differently. I came up through the theater by way of dance. I really wanted to be a ballerina, I always have, and still love the strength and grace tenacity, the ballerinas show. But when my body didn’t develop like a ballerinas, the dream had to change. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-hunter-phoenix-2)
