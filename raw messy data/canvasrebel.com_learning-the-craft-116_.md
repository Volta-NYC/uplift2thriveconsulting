One of the most daunting aspects of pursing a creative or artistic career for many aspiring artists is not knowing how to learn the craft. So, we asked some very talented artists and creatives to talk to us about how they learned their crafts and we hope their stories will help you in your journey.

## Sara Nygard![Meet Sara Nygard](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/c-1776370167549-personal_1776370166641_1776370166641_sara_nygard_img_7814.jpg)

I started taking art lessons of all sorts, and college credits even, in grade school. It was a lot of work and great fun but sometimes kids ended up going home in tears after receiving harsh critique. Our teachers back in the 80’s were professional artists and big personalities with strong opinions and no filter. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-sara-nygard)

## Joshuua Sohn

As a film composer, I learned my craft through lots of trial-and-error, studying music at the University level, taking on various projects, and through my tenure as a composer’s assistant for Christian Davis. Every project demands something unique that you can’t always apply to other projects. Sometimes, one way of writing the music will work in one scenario but it won’t work in another. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-joshuua-sohn)

## Adrianne Kimbell![Meet Adrianne Kimbell](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/c-1775785107449-personal_1775785106747_1775785106747_adrianne_kimbell_adrianne-kimbell_georgia-map-1.png)

My path was anything but a straight line. I was an English major at the University of Florida, hopeful I’d end up as an editor at a publishing house in New York City, or something glamorous like that. Instead, I became an administrative clerk at the Federal Reserve Bank in Jacksonville, Florida. Finance and business were not my thing, to be honest. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-adrianne-kimbell)

## Jonah Bokaer![Meet Jonah Bokaer](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1775718348053-personal_1775718347588_1775718347588_jonah_bokaer_199.jpg)

I was extraordinarily fortunate, to attend the UNCSA arts conservatory, ages 15-18, on scholarship, and was immediately catapulted into the Merce Cunningham Dance Company subsequently at age 18 as its youngest dancer; I then began choreographing on the scale of theatre artist Robert Wilson´s stages at age 24, as his collaborator and deputy while charting my own choreographic career. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-jonah-bokaer)

## Honest Lee Soul![Meet Honest Lee Soul](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1776118581658-personal_1776118581219_1776118581219_honestlee_soul_img_4973.jpg)

We’ll discuss the craft of songwriting. Sharing the responsibility of songwriting didn’t come together from the beginning. We started as a duo and that process was a little different than what it is now as a five member band. The first record we recorded was really stripped down and mostly acoustic. We called. in some friends to fill in the gaps. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-honest-lee-soul-2)

## Evan Friedmann![Meet Evan Friedmann](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1775759127801-personal_1775759127927_1775759127927_evan_friedmann_cybercrypt_personal.jpg)

I started by asking other soft solder artists how they got their start and what resources they suggested for beginners. This helped loads at the start, it prevented me from investing in the wrong materials needed for the craft. At first, I soldered with many different mediums, such as copper tape, wire, and foil. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-evan-friedmann)

## CJ Wise  ![](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1776070843204-personal_1776070842862_1776070842862_cj_wise_fb_img_1685546676007.jpg)

1) I’ve had a passion for storytelling since I was a child. Writing was always something I turned to…it was my way of escaping reality and creating worlds of my own. Over time, that passion naturally evolved into something more. I didn’t learn through a formal path; I learned by writing consistently, experimenting, and allowing my imagination to lead. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-cj-wise)

## Brian Metcalf![Meet Brian Metcalf](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1776022960196-personal_1776022959686_1776022959686_brian_metcalf_img_1598.jpg)

For learning all of the creative crafts I have gone into, the common thread I have focused on is research. I would study the craft that I was going into and learn how it works by talking with people who are more seasoned than myself and by watching how others who were successful in their fields did it. There have been many successes and failures. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-brian-metcalf-2)

## Kenneth P. Garnier![Meet Kenneth P. Garnier](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1776261488608-personal_1776261488353_1776261488353_kennethp_garnier_canvas-rebel-kpg-personal-pic-4-2026.jpg)

I discovered song creation as an organic form of self-expression and self-care at an early age. Because of invaluable family influence and support, my upbringing afforded me the opportunity to become familiar with multiple genres of music and study a variety of instruments. This sparked an unquenchable interest to learn about the formality of songwriting, arranging, and producing. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-kenneth-p-garnier-2)

## De’Marrco Cannon![](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1776304394536-personal_1776304394153_1776304394152_demarrco_1000004702-768x1024.jpg)

It’s been nonstop practice practice practice everyday and if not everyday whenever I can it’s always important to practice your craft ESPECIALLY when it’s something that means a lot to you . [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-demarrco-cannon)

## Tricia Cruz![Meet Tricia Cruz](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1776134096342-personal_1776134095823_1776134095823_tricia_cruz_tcruz.jpg)

I learned to do what I do through a mix of formal training, real-world experience, and a lot of observation. Acting wasn’t something I mastered overnight—it came from studying the craft, being on set, auditioning constantly, and learning from every “yes” and every “no.” I paid attention to other actors, directors, and even crew members, because storytelling is collaborative. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-tricia-cruz)
