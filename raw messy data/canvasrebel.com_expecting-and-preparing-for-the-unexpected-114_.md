Having to overcome unexpected problems is a hallmark of entrepreneurship. Almost every business owner, artist, maker, and creative we know has had to deal with countless unexpected problems that arise in the journey and so we asked some brilliant folks from the community to share their stories of the unexpected problems they’ve faced along the way.

## Frances Dupree![Meet Frances Dupree](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779106265793-personal_1779106266012_1779106266012_frances_dupree_c0eaaa59-640b-47d1-aed4-c4f92c019b4a.png)

One of the most unexpected challenges in my professional career wasn’t a system failure, a difficult implementation, or even managing competing priorities across legal operations. It was realizing that the dream I had carried for years might not be the path God actually called me to walk. For most of my life, becoming an attorney was the goal. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-frances-dupree)

## Irwin Rogers![Meet Irwin Rogers](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778976019723-personal_1778976019604_1778976019604_irwin_rogers_img_1716.jpg)

One of the most unexpected problems I faced in my professional career was realizing that strong lesson planning alone was not enough to guarantee student success. Early in my teaching journey, I believed that if I created an engaging lesson, prepared clear materials, and delivered the content with energy, students would naturally meet the objective. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-irwin-rogers-2)

## Justin Puppe![Meet Justin Puppe](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779328769285-personal_1779328770778_1779328770778_justin_puppe_pup.webp)

One of the most unexpected problems I have faced is realizing how slowly even major environmental issues move, and even when the problem itself is clear. A lot of people assume that if a contamination issue is serious enough, the system will naturally respond quickly. But in reality, environmental progress often sits in a deadlock between regulation, politics, corporate risk, funding, and public awareness. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-justin-puppe-2)

## Ryan Travis![Meet Ryan Travis](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779320840806-personal_1779320840589_1779320840589_ryan_travis_ryan-travis-director.jpg)

Filmmaking is a deeply creative endeavor on one hand, and a highly logistical, business-driven process on the other. The real work happens where those two worlds meet. Where you’re constantly problem-solving to protect the creative vision while navigating constraints, limitations, and issues outside of your control. Most of the time, it’s about finding ways around unexpected problems that inevitably arise. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-ryan-travis)

## Michelle Burke![Meet Michelle Burke](https://canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779130270455-personal_1779130270253_1779130270253_michelle_burke_untitled-design.jpg)

The worst problems hit you right when you’re finally ready to move. I was writing my book. I’d taken a few months off and had just gotten off a call with my book coach. I was fired up, ready to get back into it. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-michelle-burke-2)

## JESSICA KOPACH![Meet JESSICA KOPACH](https://canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779277477737-personal_1779277477600_1779277477600_jessica_kopach_img_1450.jpeg)

One of the most unexpected and defining challenges I’ve faced in business honestly had very little to do with marketing itself and everything to do with leadership during pressure, uncertainty, and rapid growth. People often see the glamorous side of PR and branding. They see the campaigns, celebrity collaborations, launches, magazine features, partnerships, events, and big moments online. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-jessica-kopach)
