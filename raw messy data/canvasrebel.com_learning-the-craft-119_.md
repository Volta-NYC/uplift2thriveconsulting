One of the most daunting aspects of pursing a creative or artistic career for many aspiring artists is not knowing how to learn the craft. So, we asked some very talented artists and creatives to talk to us about how they learned their crafts and we hope their stories will help you in your journey.

## Diego Ortega ![](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778983818930-1778983818716_diego_max00546.jpg)

In the beginning, it all started with the good old auto settings and taking pictures of what I thought looked good. Throughout taking more photos and just being there, I learned more and more about how to frame a photo and to catch a moment even before it happens. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-diego-ortega)

## Orianne Cosentino![Meet Orianne Cosentino](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778264307063-personal_1778264306679_1778264306679_orianne_cosentino_img_6169.jpeg)

The first professional kitchen I experienced was in Paris, France. I was living there and loved making art but did not see, at the time, how painting could financially support me. So I went to work waiting tables at a restaurant in the 11th. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-orianne-cosentino)

## Jacqui Moore![Meet Jacqui Moore](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777907969619-personal_1777907969205_1777907969205_jacqui_moore_img_2159.jpeg)

I am a self taught artist. Everything was a trial & error situation but I knew it wouldn’t always.be like that. I definitely should have listened to my younger self & went to cosmetology school after highschool. I can only imagine what my journey would have been like but better late than never. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-jacqui-moore-2)

## Esme Krahn![Meet Esme Krahn](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778530442084-personal_1778530443695_1778530443695_esme_krahn_phil0726_websize-1.jpg)

When I started in 2004, the main focus of events was logistics. Since I have a career in supply chain, it was easy for me to use those skills to create an event timeline. The goal was to make sure all vendors were informed about the setup, the timing, role clarification, etc. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-esme-krahn)

## EmmaLee Manes![Meet EmmaLee Manes](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777945986037-personal_1777945985515_1777945985515_emmalee_manes_img_0474.jpeg)

Trying to become a content creator in a world of content creators is failing or feeling like you’re failing but continuing to push forward. So I think I learned, by failing. I could have done more research of editing, camera angles, scripts, etc. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-emmalee-manes)

# Lennette Carrero![Meet Lennette Carrero](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778019272482-personal_1778019272042_1778019272042_lennette_carrero_img_2345.jpeg)

I truly learned by doing. When I started, I didn’t have a blueprint—I had a vision. I paid attention to details, studied the event industry, and learned through every setup, every client interaction, and every challenge. Over time, I realized this business isn’t just about soft play—it’s about creating an experience [. Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-lennette-carrero)

## ANGELO FERRARI![Meet ANGELO FERRARI](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778254838122-personal_1778254837147_1778254837147_angelo_ferrari_angelo-pr-foto.jpg)

I grew up in the music / entrainment business. If there is one thing that I learned is that all the great artists take the time to first learn their craft , then master their craft and continue to hone their craft. I do not think that it is a process you can speed up other than putting in more time. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-angelo-ferrari)

## Lena Báez![Meet Lena Báez](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778221117533-personal_1778221117425_1778221117425_lena_bez_img_0321.jpg)

How did you learn to do what you do? Knowing what you know now, what could you have done to speed up your learning process? What skills do you think were most essential? What obstacles stood in the way of learning more? I wouldn’t speed up my learning journey, doing so would take away the enjoyment of the process. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-lena-baez-2)

## Mariano De Luca![Meet Mariano De Luca](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777913776440-personal_1777913775920_1777913775920_mariano_deluca_img_3773.jpeg)

I went to film school for something completely different than cinematography, I wanted to create Music Scores. I play guitar since I’m 12 years old and always wanted to be a working musician. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-mariano-de-luca-3)

## Dregan Feaster![Meet Dregan Feaster](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777866761186-personal_1777866760372_1777866760372_dregan_feaster_grp_4964.jpg)

I felt my journey with photography was spiritually led and divinely orchestrated by God he allowed me to cultivate my skills and gain knowledge through different methods, forms of inspiration, people & experiences. What I could’ve done to speed up the process was focus more on my relationship with God and not getting distracted by what my plans were but trusting his plan completely. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-dregan-feaster-2)

## Bieunkah Anwojue![Meet Bieunkah Anwojue](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778603501617-personal_1778603499774_1778603499774_bieunkah_anwojue_live.jpg)

I have been drawing my entire life. As a child, I would always doodle and create cards. I loved reading books and seeing how the illustrations brought the stories to life. Getting into the book business pushed me to continue learning and working on my craft. Being able to draw, does not mean that you can bring a story to life visually. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-bieunkah-anwojue-2)

## Audra Ashlee![Meet Audra Ashlee](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778177558918-personal_1778177558814_1778177558814_audra_ashlee_img_9579.jpeg)

I really learned the art of makeup from other makeup artists. I love to watch videos of artists doing makeup to learn new trends and techniques, but I really learned how to apply those skills from working hands on with my peers. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-audra-ashlee)

## Addie Morgester![Meet Addie Morgester](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778703580204-personal_1778703580029_1778703580029_addie_morgester_artfully-overjoyed-2024-86.jpg)

I am a teacher by trade so graphic design was not something I learned in the traditional sense or got a degree in. I am self taught and began designing in a more legitimate sense roughly five years ago. I knew in order to be taken seriously as a stationer, I needed to learn design forward programs like the ones in Adobe. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-addie-morgester)

## Angelica Trygar![Meet Angelica Trygar](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778792541913-personal_1778792541746_1778792541746_angelica_trygar_img_8783.jpg)

I really learned by living through hard things and sharing honestly along the way. In 2023, one of my close friends encouraged me to start my YouTube channel, Acting Savvy. People around me had always called me a hustler because no matter how difficult life got, I always found a way to survive, adapt, and thrive. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-angelica-trygar-3)

## Daniel Fiorda![Meet Daniel Fiorda](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778780696009-personal_1778780695861_1778780695860_daniel_fiorda_d-fiorda-5.jpg)

I learned through experimentation, repetition, and long periods of observation. I was never interested in speeding up the process — for me, time is part of the formation itself. Developing a personal language requires patience, doubt, discipline, and curiosity. I think the most important skill is observation. Learning how to truly see — proportions, textures, tension, silence, imperfections, and relationships between forms. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-daniel-fiorda-2)

## Debbi James![Meet Debbi James](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778698706281-personal_1778698706107_1778698706107_debbi_james_img_9360.jpeg)

Throughout my musical journey I’ve had the opportunity to perform on big stages alongside major artists like Enrique Iglesias, Jennifer Lopez, and Anastacia. But my journey to get there wasn’t simple or fast. I moved to the United States when I was 18 years old, without speaking any English Hebrew was my first language. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-debbi-james-2)

## Beorht Lewinski![Meet Beorht Lewinski](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778715859171-personal_1778715859054_1778715859054_beorht_lewinski_img_0982.jpeg)

I learned most of what I do through survival, curiosity, and experience. At a young age, I didn’t completely know what I wanted to become — I just knew I wanted to be successful. As I got older, I started telling myself that my biggest fear was ending up feeling unsuccessful or not valuable, so I pushed myself to become good at everything I could. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-beorht-lewinski-5)

## Christian Cipollini![Meet Christian Cipollini](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778084472601-personal_1778084472097_1778084472097_christian_cipollini_img_5211.jpeg)

I am still learning! It’s a process that doesn’t have a defined beginning for me but rather a series of experiences and observations that took shape over time. It’s a process that ultimately never ends, hopefully. But, more specifically, I learned much (of elements that would ultimately be key lessons or influences to my craft and passion) growing up. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-christian-cipollini-2)

## Devaun Owens![Meet Devaun Owens](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778784329285-personal_1778784329107_1778784329107_devaun_owens_img_0261.jpeg)

I learned my craft through watching, listening, and imitating what I saw my uncles do. Music is in my family on both sides so I picked it up fairly easy. Singing was a talent I’ve always had, production however, took sometime to learn. It honestly takes a lot of patience and effort to really understand certain things. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-devaun-owens)

## Michael Gallegos![Meet Michael Gallegos](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777678881907-personal_1777678881398_1777678881398_michael_gallegos_img_2532-1.jpeg)

I learned by doing, a lot of trial and error, repetition and putting myself in environments that pushed me to make smarter choices and be faster. I went thru cosmetology school, but honestly, most of my growth came after, not being afraid to take clients right away, assisting, putting myself in rooms where I wasn’t the best yet.
