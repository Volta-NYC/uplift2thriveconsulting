Working on something meaningful is a common desire – but how? We started asking folks to share the story of how they ended up working on a project they felt was meaningful because we wanted inspiration but also because we wondered if hearing from people from across a range of industries would help us identify patterns making it more likely for anyone to be able to find and work on projects that they would find meaningful.

## Ilya Ganpantsura![Meet Ilya Ganpantsura](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778162466305-personal_1778162465273_1778162465273_ilya_ganpantsura_1777905717246.jpg)

Created with the purpose of continuing the path, the online magazine Marginalic Nova became the ideal quintessence of three things: my personal experience in journalism, the experience of building the previous magazine “Palette Essay,” and the analysis of and interaction with the Russian-speaking literary space. During the work on the architecture of Marginalic Nova, I was constantly kept under tension by the political aspect. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-ilya-ganpantsura)

## Catherine McKinley![Meet Catherine McKinley](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778256530736-personal_1778256530102_1778256530102_catherine_mckinley_890b8470-09ef-4e88-9453-2a91b87ff460.png)

One of the most meaningful aspects of my work hasn’t been a single project, but rather the ongoing commitment to using photography as a way to serve and strengthen the community around me. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-catherine-mckinley)

## Stephen Tucker![Meet Stephen Tucker](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778721667652-personal_1778721667190_1778721667190_stephen_tucker_hitw_vip-private-screening_buffalo-movie-theater_sept-11_2025_bighorn-films_0381.jpg)

The most meaningful project I’ve worked on in my entire career is the documentary project I am currently working on titled Medicine on the Mountain. We are in early stages of planning, fundraising and filming this project. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-stephen-tucker)

## Meg Britten![Meet Meg Britten](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778170971883-personal_1778170971402_1778170971402_meg_britten_meg-britten-canvas-rebel-photo.jpg)

Exactly one year ago, I had the privilege of serving as the primary artist for Ronald McDonald House Charities of Tampa Bay’s newest project at St. Joseph’s Children’s Hospital. Working alongside RMH designer Jennifer Bertrand and the incredible staff was an experience I’ll always treasure. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-meg-britten)

## Manuela Durson![Meet Manuela Durson](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778089577512-personal_1778089577021_1778089577021_manuela_durson_butte.jpg)

One of the most meaningful parts of my work has been creating landscape photography and cinematic drone videos that capture the feeling of a place, not just what it looks like. I spend a lot of time exploring the Oregon Coast, the American West, and remote natural areas, often chasing weather, light, fog, storms, or quiet moments that only exist for a few minutes. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-manuela-durson)

## Dalia Amine![Meet Dalia Amine](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778109336643-personal_1778109336243_1778109336243_dalia_amine_181321.jpg)

One of the most meaningful projects in my career has been creating cultural and educational travel experiences that connect international visitors with the true spirit of Egypt beyond the traditional tourist perspective. What began as a personal passion for archaeology and heritage gradually evolved into a mission to present Egypt through authentic storytelling, historical depth, and human connection. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-dalia-amine-2)

## Shayna Dunkelman![Meet Shayna Dunkelman](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778177605937-personal_1778177605849_1778177605849_shayna_dunkelman_shayna-dunkelman-photo-1.jpg)

The most meaningful project I have been a part of is NOMON, the percussion duo I share with my sister, Nava. Working alongside family brings a profound depth to the creative process, and it has served as a massive catalyst for my broader artistic adventures. What started as an exploration of shared rhythm has evolved into a space where we combine entire ecosystems of creation. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-shayna-dunkelman-2)

## Zoe Rogers![Meet Zoe Rogers](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1774710923752-personal_1774710923354_1774710923354_zoe_rogers_bcf-group-1.jpeg)

Creating Boulder Comedy Festival was really meaningful because it highlights women and diversity which was something really lacking when I first stated about 15 years ago. It meant a lot to have the support of the Venues like Dairy Arts and Junkyard Social. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-zoe-rogers-4)

## Brian Morales![Meet Brian Morales](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777953956334-personal_1777953955634_1777953955634_brian_morales_dsc00062.jpeg)

Last year, Mark Shapiro — artistic director at Cantor New York — commissioned me to create a chamber version of Adolphus Hailstork’s large-scale cantata Crispus Attucks. The piece (originally written in 2007) had felt like it was on the verge of a real renaissance after the George Floyd protests, but laid surprisingly dormant. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-brian-morales)

## Crystal Wimbrey![Meet Crystal Wimbrey](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1776396585968-personal_1776396585282_1776396585282_crystal_wimbrey_92715.webp)

Both my book series collaboration and the Mother & Daughter Brunch & Bond event hold deep meaning for me because I’m blessed to share these intentional movements alongside my daughters. Moments like these remind me that legacy is built through love, purpose, and togetherness. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-crystal-wimbrey-2)

## Gracie Yates![Meet Gracie Yates](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1776284935239-personal_1776284934824_1776284934824_gracie_yates_img_5330_vsco.jpeg)

One of the most meaningful projects I’m currently working on is recording my next music project while preparing for a summer of festival performances that once felt like far-off dreams. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-gracie-yates-2)

## Hickman L.A.![Meet Hickman L.A.](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1775395982424-personal_1775395982022_1775395982022_hickmanla_img_7888.jpeg)

This project came from a real place in my life where everything was changing at once — personally, professionally, and emotionally. I was dealing with growth in my career, shifting relationships, stepping into more responsibility within my family, and really trying to understand who I was becoming in the middle of all of it. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-hickman-l-a-2)

## Dorian Davis![Meet Dorian Davis](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778038973508-personal_1778038972887_1778038972887_dorian_davis_inaribriana_dorian_davis_final-2.jpg)

The most meaningful project I’ve ever worked on is the one I’m preparing to release now — my first short film, Moonchild. This film is the culmination of a six‑year journey that began long before I ever picked up a camera. It’s the extension of my premiere art collection, Man on the Moon, which I started in 2020 in my apartment in Washington, DC. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-dorian-davis-2)

## Anna Groos![Meet Anna Groos](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777855707328-personal_1777855706804_1777855706804_anna_groos_img_3656.jpeg)

One of the most meaningful projects I’ve worked on wasn’t a large campaign or a high-budget shoot. It was a family session that turned into something much deeper. I had the opportunity to photograph a couple while the husband was battling terminal brain cancer. Their daughter had set up the session while, being states away, so they would have the memories. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-anna-groos-2)

## Pri the Honeydark![Meet Pri The Honeydark](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778384621917-personal_1778384620094_1778384620094_pri_thehoneydark_pri-brownie-headshot.jpg)

Without question, the most meaningful project I’ve worked on has been my portrait series “Pass the Crown.” The project was inspired by my Nana while I was caring for her during her dementia journey. Watching someone you love slowly lose pieces of their memory is incredibly painful, but I also became very aware of how people living with dementia often become invisible to society. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-pri-the-honeydark)

## Michaela Miller![Meet Michaela Miller](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777837721465-personal_1777837721005_1777837721005_michaela_miller_studio-session-1653.jpg)

Other than the projects I’ve created myself, which are all very meaningful to me, I can’t pick one child, it would be the production I did of ‘She Kills Monsters’ in my last semester of college. It’s so meaningful because of how kismet it felt. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-michaela-miller-2)

## Blake Red![](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777954077268-personal_1777954076487_1777954076487_blake_red_blakered_photo7byvictoriamarie-768x512.jpg)

My third studio EP, ‘Out of the Cage’ is my most meaningful project to date. The timing of the release was really meaningful for me because it had been years since I put out new music. During those few years one of my biggest supporters, my dad, became very ill with cancer and I witnessed the fight for his life. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-blake-red)

## Eli Gabrieloff![Meet Eli Gabrieloff](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777866097804-personal_1777866097285_1777866097285_eli_gabrieloff_img_1463.jpeg)

One of the most rewarding parts of this work is building instruments for the musicians I work with — guitars made exactly to their specs, exactly the way they need them. One that stands out is a guitar I built for a close friend who tours the world with a major rock band. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-eli-gabrieloff)

## Florencio Lennox Campello![Meet Florencio Lennox Campello](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778597867365-personal_1778597866634_1778597866634_florenciolennox_campello_cba.jpg)

Over 20 years ago I was on NPR radio discussing the lack of proportional representation of women artists in most museums worldwide. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-florencio-lennox-campello)

## Calvin Walker![Meet Calvin Walker](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778468043181-personal_1778468091421_1778468091421_calvin_walker_edited-18.jpg)

The most meaningful project I ever worked on was a mini-doc/campaign video – I’m not really sure what to call it – that I directed and produced for my dear friend Nono Osuji. Nono is an amazing actress and writer that I met in the pandemic days. She hosted a podcast with a dear friend of mine, Nikki Dixon. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-calvin-walker-2)

## Carlie Hilbert![Meet Carlie Hilbert](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778597420718-personal_1778597420656_1778597420656_carlie_hilbert_img_5397.jpeg)

I was chosen to be a heart artist for the Parade of Hearts in Kansas City Missouri. Over 700 people applied and only 150 were chosen. For my heart sculpture I incorporated 12 pet portraits of dogs that I have been commissioned to paint and gave each dog a banana, collar, or bow tie with something that symbolizes Kansas City. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-carlie-hilbert)

## Amber Peters![Meet Amber Peters](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778638377213-personal_1778638376337_1778638376336_amber_peters_img_63491.jpg)

One of the most meaningful parts of my work has been bringing a variety of community events into the garden and helping it grow into a true space of connection, healing, and engagement. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-amber-peters)

## Wendy Nielsen![Meet Wendy Nielsen](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778264306003-personal_1778264305722_1778264305722_wendy_nielsen_img_9964-1.jpg)

This could also go under ‘taking a risk,’ but I have scaled back on my photographic work. I no longer ‘chase jobs,’ and I only reach out to potential clients or businesses if it is something I truly want to do. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-wendy-nielsen-2)

## David Shopper![Meet David Shopper](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778681699056-personal_1778681698812_1778681698812_david_shopper_david_shopper_web.jpg)

I don’t know if this is my most meaningful project, but it’s certainly the latest, as I’m working on it right now. I’m helping a global property company that designs and manages commercial real estate brand their company. Their CEO was tired of using generic stock imagery on their website and proposals. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-david-shopper-2)

## Tanner Sloss![Meet Tanner Sloss](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778278350022-personal_1778278349937_c656f097-5f96-47d1-ba0c-f910c22be066.jpg)

In March of 2023, a school shooting at the Covenant School in Nashville took six lives, three of them only nine years old. I grew up in the South, where conversations around gun violence were already part of my life from a young age. But this time, it felt different. It felt personal. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-tanner-sloss)

## Dennis Linggar![Meet Dennis Linggar](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777962447812-personal_1777962447621_1777962447621_dennis_dennis-linggar-by-miguel-8.jpg)

One of the most meaningful projects I’ve worked on is my song Jersey Heartbreak. This song was written in a season where I was processing heartbreak in a very real and personal way. This song portrays the the confusion, the frustration, and the question of whether it’s even worth opening yourself up to love again. I didn’t want to just write a typical breakup song. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-dennis-linggar)

## Alison Auditore![Meet Alison Auditore](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777038006594-personal_1777038011519_1777038011519_alison_auditore_639083065512730000.jpg)

The series I’m working on right now is the most meaningful one I’ve worked on. Every decade has its struggles and tragedies, but this feels like an especially tumultuous time. As Nina Simone said, it’s an artist’s duty to reflect the times, so I am trying to fulfill that obligation and use art to document our societal reaction to world events. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-alison-auditore-2)

## ashlee hayden![Meet Ashlee Hayden](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777417379118-personal_1777417378613_1777417378613_ashlee_hayden_photographer-oc-one-year-pictures181.jpg)

Honestly, the most meaningful work I do is my maternity sessions. I’ve worked with so many women who come in feeling unsure of themselves—sometimes uncomfortable in their changing bodies, sometimes just not feeling like “themselves” anymore. And I get that… pregnancy is beautiful, but it can also feel really vulnerable. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-ashlee-hayden-2)

## Hannah Bennett![Meet Hannah Bennett](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1772987591634-personal_1772987591107_1772987591107_hannah_bennett_bennett01jpg.webp)

One of the experiences I had during the years before I went back to making art was living in Ecuador as an agriculture volunteer in the Peace Corps. I’d really wanted to spend significant time not traveling but living in a different country, and this was a way to do it. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-hannah-bennett)

## Shirin Delalat![Meet Shirin Delalat](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1776774405421-personal_1776774405321_1776774405321_shirin_sd-62_a.jpeg)

i wish i was worse’ is the most meaningful thing I’ve made. It’s a memoir in essays about the exhausting math of likability. The silent calculations women run constantly about how much honesty is allowed, how much is too much, and who we lose when we tell the truth. No redemption arc. No lessons. No arriving anywhere improved. The catalyst was losing my closest friends. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-shirin-delalat)

## Shuai Gong![Woman smiling and holding a microphone in a classroom, with a man standing nearby and children seated.](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1775988578850-1775988577496_shuai_gong_3.jpeg)

Among the projects I have participated in, the “Made in Kyrgyzstan” National Brand Project remains one of the most meaningful to me. The project grew out of a 12-day international exchange program, and what made it especially memorable was that I was not simply designing products, but entering a living cultural context. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-shuai-gong)

## Grace Whitworth![Meet Grace Whitworth](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1773155254702-personal_1773155253877_1773155253876_grace_whitworth_image-3-18-25-at-514-pm.jpeg)

I became a Mother in 2020. I found out I was pregnant in January and then the whole world shut down in March. I do believe that the isolation, the unknown, the sheer panic and divisiveness of that time gave way to my diagnosis of postpartum anxiety/depression. On top of that, my sole identity as a dancer was transitioning. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-grace-whitworth)
