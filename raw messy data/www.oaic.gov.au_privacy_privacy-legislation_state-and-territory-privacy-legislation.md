# State and territory privacy legislation

The Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) mainly deals with issues that are covered by the [_Privacy Act 1988_ \- external site](http://www.legislation.gov.au/Series/C2004A03712). The Privacy Act is a federal law which does not cover local, state or territory government agencies, except the Norfolk Island administration.

Most Australian states and territories have equivalent legislation which covers their public sector agencies. Some [state authorities and instrumentalities](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/privacy/the-privacy-act/rights-and-responsibilities#who-has-responsibilities-under-the-privacy-act) are bound by the Privacy Act.

If your inquiry or complaint is about a New South Wales (NSW), Queensland, Northern Territory, Tasmanian or Victorian public sector agency, including a local council, please contact the relevant commissioner using the links below.

## Private sector health service providers

The Privacy Act applies to all private sector [health service providers](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/privacy/health-information/what-is-a-health-service-provider) anywhere in Australia. It doesn’t apply to state and territory public sector health service providers, such as public hospitals.

In NSW, Victoria and the Australian Capital Territory (ACT) private sector health service providers must comply with both Australian and state or territory privacy laws when handling [health information](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/privacy/health-information/what-is-health-information).

Queensland, the Northern Territory and Tasmania have privacy legislation that applies only to their public sector, including public sector health service providers.

Western Australia and South Australia do not have specific privacy legislation.

## Australian Capital Territory

The [ACT Privacy Commissioner \- external site](https://www.hrc.act.gov.au/__data/assets/image/0004/2287948/Whole-of-Commission-org-structure.png) administers the _[Information Privacy Act 2014 \- external site](https://www.legislation.act.gov.au/a/2014-24)_ (ACT) and the ACT Human Rights Commission accepts complaints about the handling of personal information by an Australian Capital Territory (ACT) agency.

Prior to the appointment of the [ACT Privacy Commissioner \- external site](https://www.hrc.act.gov.au/__data/assets/image/0004/2287948/Whole-of-Commission-org-structure.png) on 1 July 2024, the OAIC provided privacy services to the ACT under a Memorandum of Understanding. Past MOUs can be found here \[link: [Past MOUs \- external site](https://webarchive.nla.gov.au/awa/20190509024107/https:/www.oaic.gov.au/about-us/corporate-information/mous)\]. The [ACT Health Services Commissioner \- external site](http://hrc.act.gov.au/health) handles health record privacy complaints.

## New South Wales

The [NSW Information and Privacy Commission \- external site](http://www.ipc.nsw.gov.au/) administers the [_Privacy and Personal Information Protection Act 1998_(NSW) \- external site](https://www.legislation.nsw.gov.au/#/view/act/1998/133) and [_Health Records and Information Privacy Act 2002_(NSW) \- external site](https://www.legislation.nsw.gov.au/#/view/act/2002/71).

## Northern Territory

The [Office of the Information Commissioner Northern Territory \- external site](https://infocomm.nt.gov.au/) oversees the privacy provisions of the [_Information Act 2002_ (NT) \- external site](https://legislation.nt.gov.au/en/Legislation/INFORMATION-ACT-2002) and accepts complaints from consumers relating to the privacy of health information.

The [Health and Community Services Complaints Commission \- external site](http://www.hcscc.nt.gov.au/about) also accepts complaints about health, disability and aged services in the Northern Territory.

## Queensland

The [Queensland Office of the Information Commissioner \- external site](http://www.oic.qld.gov.au/) receives privacy complaints under the [_Information Privacy Act 2009_ (Qld) \- external site](https://www.legislation.qld.gov.au/view/html/inforce/current/act-2009-014) which covers the Queensland public sector.

Queensland’s [Health Ombudsman \- external site](http://www.oho.qld.gov.au/) can also receive and investigate complaints about health services and health service providers.

## South Australia

The [South Australian privacy committee \- external site](https://www.archives.sa.gov.au/privacy-committee/privacy-committee-of-south-australia "Privacy Committee of South Australia website") handles privacy complaints related to state government agencies’ compliance with a set of Information Privacy Principles

The [Health and Community Services Complaints Commissioner \- external site](http://www.hcscc.sa.gov.au/) also receives complaints about government, private and non-government health and community services.

## Tasmania

The [Tasmanian Ombudsman \- external site](http://www.ombudsman.tas.gov.au/) accepts complaints in relation to the [_Personal Information and Protection Act 2004_ (Tas) \- external site](https://www.legislation.tas.gov.au/view/html/inforce/current/act-2004-046). This legislation covers the Tasmanian public sector, including public hospitals.

## Victoria

The [Office of the Victorian Information Commissioner \- external site](https://www.ovic.vic.gov.au/) (OVIC) administers the [_Privacy and Data Protection Act 2014_ (Vic) \- external site](http://www8.austlii.edu.au/cgi-bin/viewdb/au/legis/vic/consol_act/padpa2014271).

The [Office of the Health Services Commissioner \- external site](http://www.health.vic.gov.au/hsc/index.htm) administers the [_Health Records Act 2001_ (Vic) \- external site](http://www6.austlii.edu.au/cgi-bin/viewdb/au/legis/vic/consol_act/hra2001144) and conciliates complaints between consumers and health care providers.

## Western Australia

The [Office of the Information Commissioner (WA) \- external site](https://www.oic.wa.gov.au/) administers the [_Freedom of Information Act 1992_ (WA) \- external site](https://www.legislation.wa.gov.au/legislation/statutes.nsf/law_a290.html) and the [Privacy and Information Sharing Act 2024 \- external site](https://www.legislation.wa.gov.au/legislation/statutes.nsf/law_a147470.html&view=consolidated), which comes into effect on 1 July 2026.

The [Health and Disability Services Complaints Office \- external site](https://www.hadsco.wa.gov.au/home) is an independent statutory authority that also handles complaints relating to health and disability services in Western Australia.
