Experience is often valued because of the lessons prior jobs have theoretically provided us with. While hearing about those lessons isn’t a replacement for actually experiencing it, we thought it would be very helpful to create a space where sharp and generous members of the community can come together to share stories and lessons learned at prior jobs for the benefit of others.

## Maura Allard![Meet Maura Allard](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778338773810-personal_1778338773921_1778338773921_maura_allard_photobycorinna-7-of-7.jpg)

I struggled with math in high school and one of the conditions to be accepted to the state university where I lived was to take a remedial math class. I felt stupid and I labeled myself as someone who could NOT do math. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-maura-allard-3)

## Michelle Machado![Meet Michelle Machado](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777580809442-personal_1777580809024_1777580809023_michelle_machado_mich_dmachado.png)

I stepped into a senior leadership role immediately after relocating from the UK to Canada at the height of the pandemic. I was now responsible for a team of over ten people I had never met in person, leading entirely remotely, in a new country, during one of the most disorienting seasons in recent history. Each of those factors alone would be a challenge. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-michelle-machado)

## Christina Boudreaux  ![Meet Christina Boudreaux](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1776878940881-personal_1776878940035_1776878940035_christina_boudreaux__dsc7238.jpg)

The best lessons have also often come as the most surprising ones. For example – As a nanny, I learned how to change a diaper (I truly did not know how until after my first day on the job. Very hands on learning!) and that maybe, just maybe, I did want kids one day. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-christina-boudreaux-2)

## Chris Reinecke![Meet Chris Reinecke](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1775512234374-personal_1775512233738_1775512233738_chris_reinecke_51760.jpeg)

One of the most important jobs I have learned in the workforce is that job security is not guaranteed. Many of us were taught by previous generations that loyalty meant clocking in on time regularly, working hard, and truly applying yourself; and that this loyalty would be rewarded. However, businesses are only ever loyal to the bottom line, and to their shareholders. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-chris-reinecke-2)
