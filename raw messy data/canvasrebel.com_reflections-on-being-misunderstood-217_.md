Often, those who tread off the beaten path are misunderstood or mischaracterized and so we asked folks from the community to reflect and tell us about the times they’ve been misunderstood or mischaracterized.

## Yuqian Zhang![Meet Yuqian Zhang](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779150883794-personal_1779150883583_1779150883583_yuqian_zhang_bcd67cc6-86ae-471b-a32e-f2164b27a0cc.jpg)

I think one of the most interesting misunderstandings around me and my work is that, for a long time, both other people and I misunderstood what cinematography could be. When I first started studying film, I was in a film program inside an art school. We studied art history and aesthetic theory seriously, but in terms of filmmaking, everyone was developing very freely. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-yuqian-zhang)

## Natsumi Goldfish![Meet Natsumi Goldfish](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779047707694-personal_1779047707472_1779047707472_natsumi_goldfish_img_5976-2-3.png)

Especially before the recent pandemic—or around 2011, when I first began painting windows that allow viewers to look into interior spaces and established them as a central theme in my work—some people commented that the paintings made them feel isolated, or asked whether I myself felt isolated from society. My window paintings go against the conventional idea of windows. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-natsumi-goldfish-2)

## Harumi Hirata![Meet Harumi  Hirata](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778991311766-personal_1778991311461_1778991311461_harumi_hirata_copy-of-lbs_2376.jpg)

During the research process, my work was occasionally misinterpreted as simply pushing for ‘accuracy’ or reconstructing culture in a strictly conventional or historical manner; at that time, Japanese, which is my identity. However, my research was never intended to police representation or discourage creative reinterpretation. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-harumi-hirata)
