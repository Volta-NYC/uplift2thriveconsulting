## [Guide to Fiber Intensive: Valais Blacknose](https://www.magcloud.com/browse/issue/3276097)

“The world’s cutest sheep”: When Valais Blacknose photos started appearing on the internet, it was hard to believe they were real: they looked like stuffed animals. They are, of course, quite real and very pleasant animals. This document looks at the breed’s source environment in Switzerland and the means by which it has been introduced to the United States, initially by upgrading or upbreeding and more recently by embryo transplant. Valais Blacknose wool varies from pleasant to crispy.

Printed document plus free digital version; 8x8" publication. This document contains theory and practical knowledge for fiber arts enthusiasts of all skill levels. The handouts are heavily illustrated.

## [Guide to Fiber Intensive: Perendale](https://www.magcloud.com/browse/issue/3276100)

The Perendale is one of three breeds developed to handle specific New Zealand environments, in this case the dry hills of the North Island. (The breeds that originated on the South Island are the Coopworth and the Corriedale.) Sir Geoffrey Peren of what is now Massey University directed their origin, and has an overall remarkable story. The sheep grow lovely mid-grade wools.
