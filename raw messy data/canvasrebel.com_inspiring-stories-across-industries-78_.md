Over the years as we connected with incredible from folks from almost every imaginable industry we realized that every nook and cranny of our economy is full of small business owners, artists and creatives who live and breathe what they do – and their stories are nothing short of heroic. It’s the immigrant parents who overcame the odds and kept their small business alive for decades allowing their next generation to thrive and the artisans who pour their passion into every piece and refuse to let even the most common items remain mundane that make our communities come alive. The stories below will inspire you and we hope you’ll take the time to read and connect with these incredible folks.

I knew once I began painting that I wanted to be an artist, and I wanted to help people. I had a deep sense that artwork is inherently healing, whether through the creative process itself or through the connection a person can experience while viewing a piece of art. ## Dr. Shenequa Miller![Meet Dr. Shenequa Miller](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1774508247643-personal_1774508247025_1774508247025_drshenequa_miller_img_6311-2.jpg)

One of the biggest investments I made early on — and honestly one of the toughest lessons — was learning how to manage costs in an industry I was still getting to know. When I first started SCM Integrity First Companies, I didn’t always know if I was paying too much for labor, materials, or services. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-dr-shenequa-miller)

## Rebecca Stephens![Meet Rebecca Stephens](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778072645143-personal_1778072644671_1778072644671_rebecca_stephens_at8a0416-edit-edit.jpg)

“Stop trying to fix the story. Get your nervous system safe first. Then make decisions from that state.” A client came in high functioning on the outside –> successful, self-aware, doing all the “right” things, but internally they were living with constant pressure and disregulation. Their mind was always working, overthinking, analyzing. Their body felt like it was bracing for impact. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-rebecca-stephens)

## Tiffany Marshall![Meet Tiffany Marshall](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778794069269-personal_1778794068934_1778794068934_tiffany_marshall_tm325-191.jpg)

For much of my early healing journey, the wellness industry heavily emphasized “fixing” yourself. More information. More certifications. More coping tools. More optimization. Healing became something women endlessly consumed while still quietly abandoning themselves, and overwhelming their holistic being, underneath it all. Over time, I watched the entire conversation begin to shift. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-tiffany-marshall)
