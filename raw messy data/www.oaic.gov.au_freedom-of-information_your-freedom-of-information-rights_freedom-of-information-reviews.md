# Freedom of information reviews

## If you’re not happy with an agency or minister’s decision in response to your freedom of information (FOI) request, you can ask for a review of the decision. You can ask for an internal review or Information Commissioner review.

You may not be happy, for example, with an agency or minister’s decision:

- not to give you [access](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/freedom-of-information/your-foi-rights/access-information-under-foi) to the document you requested or in the form you requested

- to [charge](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/freedom-of-information/how-to-make-an-foi-request/what-charges-may-apply) you for access to the document you requested or the amount of the charge

- to refuse to [amend or annotate](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/freedom-of-information/your-foi-rights/correct-your-personal-information-under-foi) a document you think is incomplete, incorrect, out of date or misleading

- to [give others access to a document](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/freedom-of-information/your-foi-rights/when-an-foi-request-affects-you) you think should not be released because it contains your [personal](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/privacy/your-privacy-rights/your-personal-information/what-is-personal-information) or business information

- to give access to a document about your physical or mental health through a qualified individual rather than directly to you.
