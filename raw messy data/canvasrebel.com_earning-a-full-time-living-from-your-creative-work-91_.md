We’ve seen way too many talented creatives quit because they couldn’t make it work financially. No doubt, the financial challenges of pursuing a creative or artistic career are daunting, but we felt there wasn’t enough discussion around how to make it work. So, we connected with artists and creatives who’ve been able to earn a full time living from their creative work and asked them to share their stories with our readers.

## Jose Perdomo III![Meet Jose Perdomo III](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778697427291-personal_1778697424959_1778697424959_jose_perdomoiii_jose-perdomo-iii.jpg)

Yes, and honestly it took longer than I expected but it happened more naturally than I planned. I started in 1998 in the Dominican Republic doing graphic design and commercial photography, which paid the bills but never really fed the soul the way I needed. The turning point was 2008 when a friend asked me to shoot her wedding. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-jose-perdomo-iii)

## Gabe Herrera![Meet Gabe Herrera](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778026265941-personal_1778026265235_1778026265235_gabe_herrera_beauxblakemillerwedding-227.jpg)

Yeah, full-time. And I’ll be honest, it still hits me sometimes that this is my actual life. My first ‘creative job’ was working in the print department at OfficeMax. Graphic design, print jobs, and learning how to make things look good on a deadline. From there, I spent almost 7 years as the senior graphic designer at HCTC, serving as their creative director. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-gabe-herrera-2)

## Heidi Fahrenbacher![Meet Heidi Fahrenbacher](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778247745845-personal_1778247745753_1778247745753_heidi_fahrenbacher_heidi-fahrenbacher-2026.jpg)

I do make a full-time living from my creative work, meaning I can pay my bills and still have money leftover to save. I say that because there is a difference between making a full-time living from your creative work and doing your work full-time. I did ceramics full-time for years, but I can honestly say I wasn’t earning a full-time living from it. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-heidi-fahrenbacher)

## Jessica Law-Davis![Meet Jessica Law-Davis](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777947620114-personal_1777947619660_1777947619660_jessica_lawdavis_jessica-headshot.png)

Yes—but it definitely wasn’t like that from day one. I started out balancing creative work with a 9–5 while I learned the industry and built relationships. The turning point came when I started treating it like a business—being reliable, moving fast, and making strong connections. That led to repeat opportunities and allowed me to branch into both acting and production, which helped stabilize my income. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-jessica-law-davis)

## Anthony Beecham![](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778005049370-personal_1778005048906_1778005048906_anthony_beecham_img_1302-768x1014.jpeg)

Yes, I have been able to earn a full time living from my creative work. It took years before this would come to fruition. I endured working for free for years honing in on my craft and rigorous practice day in and out to become a good producer and professional. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-anthony-beecham)

## Chris Harry![Meet Chris Harry](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778071280084-personal_1778071279653_1778071279653_chris_harry_e3765864-e402-41b3-8bc6-31035fb56d5b.jpeg)

I’ve been able to build a life where creative work is a meaningful part of my income and identity, but I still maintain a full-time career in accounting. Music has never felt like an overnight success story for me — it’s been a long game built on consistency, patience, and growth. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-chris-harry)

## Cat Tesla![Meet Cat Tesla](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778619104503-personal_1778619104384_1778619104384_cat_tesla_new-moon.jpg)

In a world where art often serves as both escape and mirror, Cat Tesla brings a powerful presence with her nature-inspired abstract compositions that invite contemplation, healing, and connection. Born in 1963 in St. Louis, Missouri, Tesla’s early life revealed a compelling duality: she excelled in both science and art, earning awards for her visual work in high school while simultaneously showing academic prowess. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-cat-tesla-2)

## Raymond Karago![Meet Raymond Karago](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778328309867-personal_1778328309396_1778328309396_raymond_karago_img_9445.jpg)

It’s been a journey, especially since moving back to my home country. However, lately things have turned around and I have been able to do various projects that pay enough for me to sustain myself and with more on the way. Networking has helped but also maintaining a reputation as a hard working professional has opened doors. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-raymond-karago-2)

## Ashley Di Si![Meet Ashley Di Si](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778354715040-personal_1778354714905_1778354714905_ashley_disi_dsc_9486.jpg)

No, it definitely wasn’t full-time from day one. I officially stepped into photography full time in December of 2023 after years of simultaneously running my business while also working in early education. Teaching was something I loved and was good at for as long as I can remember. I always knew that in some way, teaching was part of my calling. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-ashley-di-si)

## Lydia Stern![Meet Lydia Stern](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777854431360-personal_1777854430875_1777854430875_lydia_stern_insta-camping-starry-sky-rooftop-tent-milky-way-astrophotography-photo-by-mountain-magic-media.jpeg)

Yes, and it’s been a journey we’re genuinely proud of, even the messy parts. Lydia started with photography as a passion, then pursued it academically after switching from a biology major (the love of earth sciences never really left — it just found a new home in the mountains). [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-lydia-stern)

## Amy M. Le![Meet Amy M. Le](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778777274794-personal_1778777274749_1778777274749_amym_le_owfi-2026.jpg)

For me, creative work and business-building have always been deeply connected. I’m an author, but I’m also the founder of Quill Hawk Publishing (https://www.quillhawkpublishing.com), so my journey wasn’t simply about selling books; it was about creating an ecosystem around storytelling, publishing, and helping other voices be heard. That absolutely did not happen overnight. In the beginning, like many creatives, I wore multiple hats. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-amy-m-le-2)

## Emily Davidson![Meet Emily Davidson](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778780300656-personal_1778780300507_1778780300507_emily_davidson_99d7fcb5-51f0-4d86-9c5a-dfcdd22e169c.jpg)

As a working musician, I’ve had to evolve, adapt, and constantly re-evaluate my personal and professional priorities. After completing my Master’s in Cello Performance in Boston, I focused on teaching private cello students to start an immediate source of income. Teaching is rewarding because it allows for mentorship and one-on-one connection in a way that live performance doesn’t.  [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-emily-davidson)

## Keston De Coteau![Meet Keston De Coteau](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778772424742-personal_1778772426531_1778772426531_keston_decoteau_screenshot-2025-05-31-173749.jpg)

I am a Baltimore-based filmmaker, photographer, and producer known for my narrative-driven visual storytelling. He is the Founder and CEO of Keystone Productions, a multimedia firm that creates commercial, documentary, and brand-focused content. Professional Background With over a decade of experience in the creative industry, he has built a reputation for high-impact visual production. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-keston-de-coteau)

## Morgan Jones![Meet Morgan Jones](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778779301745-personal_1778779301521_1778779301521_morgan_jones_head-shot-toaf.jpg)

Earning a full-time living from creative work definitely didn’t happen overnight for me. For many years I worked out of a small attached garage in Leslieville, Toronto, slowly developing my style through experimentation and trial and error. There were some really tough years in the beginning. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-morgan-jones-4)

## Kadir Mayel![Meet Kadir Mayel](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1776897293429-personal_1776897292919_1776897292919_kadir_mayel_img_3159.jpeg)

Yes — I’ve been able to build a full-time living from my creative work, but it definitely didn’t happen overnight. I didn’t come from a traditional film background. I actually started as an engineer, and at some point I made the decision to walk away from stability and pursue filmmaking seriously. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-kadir-mayel)

## Tre G![Meet Tre G](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1776883015306-personal_1776883014885_1776883014885_tre_g_ded_7290.jpeg)

Nothing about the journey was smooth. My mom, Gloria Jean Freeman, was a single mother doing everything she could to provide for us. We moved around a lot trying to find stability, but life had a way of shaking things up. She was a true warrior. She married my dad before I was born, and they divorced shortly after. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-tre-g)

## Michael Dickson-Mills and Jose Noya![Meet Michael Dickson-Mills And Jose Noya](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1777039694442-personal_1777039694037_1777039694037_michaeldicksonmillsjosenoya_na_beech-bluff-01.jpg)

We started Notch Design after working together and realizing we shared a similar design approach. Early on, like most small practices, we relied on relationships—friends, past clients, and collaborators who trusted us and were familiar with our work. Those first projects were enough to get us started, but not immediately enough to sustain a full-time practice. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-michael-dickson-mills-and-jose-noya)
