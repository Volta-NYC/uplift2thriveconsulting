Risk is a topic that comes up a lot in our conversations with entrepreneurs and creatives, but a related topic is investments. Investments come in many forms – investing your time, money, heart and soul, etc. So, with that broad definition of investment in mind, we asked rising stars in the community to tell us the stories of the best or worst investments they’ve made.

## Camille Cabrera  ![](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/c-1773109015007-personal_1773109014216_1773109014216_camille_cabrera_img_2897.png)

The best investment was always going to be turning my passion into talent. I wouldn’t say I started out as an amazing writer. Honestly? I really struggled to learn how to read. It felt like it took me ages to get on equal footing with the rest of my class. But I kept trying. [Read more>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-camille-cabrera-2)

## Grace Onuegbu  ![](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/c-1774988372115-personal_1774988371589_1774988371589_grace_onuegbu_main-picture.jpg)

Before I had the full resource, The best investment I’ve made as a business owner was betting on myself early on. In 2018, I heard a coach share her story and was inspired by her transformation. I wanted to work with her but couldn’t afford it at the time. I made a promise that when the opportunity came, I would say yes. [Read more>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-grace-onuegbu-2)

## Iss Doja  ![](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/c-1775060023818-personal_1775060023424_1775060023424_iss_doja_img_2823.png)

Great question! My best investment was myself and getting the llc for the brand Kartoon Blvd. honestly when it comes to music there aren’t really too many investments or I should say good investments. people will come to you and offer services but you then have to keep questioning if the service are legit. [Read more>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-iss-doja)

## Sam Lund  ![](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/c-1774588039079-personal_1774588038332_1774588038332_sam_lund_lund_grandamerica-11331-wuqstvj2uwppbzq3igmedq-cpxiojlbq1igilkdxh5arq.webp)

Honestly? It’s always people. Every single time, the best investments I’ve ever made and the worst investments I’ve ever made have been people. Not software. Not marketing. Not equipment. People. Early on I made the mistake a lot of business owners make,I hired for skillset. Someone looked great on paper, had the experience, could do the thing I needed done. [Read more>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-sam-lund)

## Geraldine Convento  ![](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/c-1774825138912-personal_1774825138346_1774825138346_geraldine_convento_geraldine-convento-entrepreneur-life-and-business-alignment-facilitator.jpg)

What’s the best or worst investment you’ve made as a business owner (either in terms of time or money)? Give us the backstory/context so we can understand what was going on, why you made the decision you did and tell us how it played out, any lessons learned, etc Although I’ve run multiple businesses for over 20 years, cash flow management wasn’t always my strength.
