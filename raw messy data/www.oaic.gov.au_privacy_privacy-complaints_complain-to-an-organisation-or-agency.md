If you think an organisation or agency has mishandled your [personal information](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/privacy/your-privacy-rights/your-personal-information/what-is-personal-information), you must complain to them first, before you complain to us.

Check their [privacy policy](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/privacy/your-privacy-rights/what-is-a-privacy-policy) — it will explain what you need to do to make a complaint. The privacy policy will also include the preferred way in which you can make a complaint ie, in writing, in person, over the phone, and will include the contact details for a privacy officer that you can direct your complaint to.

## What to include in your privacy complaint

When you make the complaint, make sure you:

- provide your name
- give any identification or reference number(s), if relevant
- give a description of the matter and why you think the organisation or agency has mishandled your personal information (what happened, when it happened and any consequences)
- let them know what you’d like them to do to resolve the matter.

If your complaint is in writing it should also include:

- a contact address
- a contact phone number
- the date.

## Privacy complaint template

You may want to use this template to make your complaint.

Dear Privacy Officer

I am writing to you to make a privacy complaint, about how \[name of agency/organisation\] has handled my personal information.

On \[date\]...\[provide an explanation of what happened, including as much detail as possible\].

As a result of this…\[explain the impact the incident has had on you and why you are concerned about this\].

To resolve this complaint, I would like your organisation to...\[outline what you are seeking to resolve the complaint\].

Please call me on \[your phone number\] to discuss the complaint.

If I do not receive a response from \[name of agency/organisation\] within a reasonable time (generally 30 days) or the complaint is not resolved, I may contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) to make a privacy complaint.

Yours sincerely

\[Your name\]

## Keep a record of your privacy complaint

Make sure you keep a record of your complaint. You will need to provide a copy to us if you want to lodge a complaint with us.

If you made your complaint in writing, keep a copy of your complaint.

If you complained in person or over the phone, you should make a record of this:

- the date you complained
- the name of the organisation or agency you complained to
- the name of the person you complained to, if available
- a description of the matter you complained about and why you think the organisation or agency has mishandled your personal information (what happened, when it happened and any consequences)
- a description of what you asked the organisation or agency to do to resolve the matter.

## Give them at least 30 days to respond

You need to give the organisation or agency a reasonable amount of time to respond to your complaint. We think 30 days is a reasonable time.

If the organisation or agency doesn’t respond to your complaint or you’re not happy with their response, you can [lodge a complaint](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/privacy/privacy-complaints/lodge-a-privacy-complaint-with-us) with us.

## Keep a record of any responses

If you receive a response from the organisation or agency in person or by phone, you may like to record (in your mobile device or diary):

- the date you received the response
- the name of the organisation or agency that responded
- the name of the person you spoke to, if available
- a description of the organisation or agency’s response.

If the organisation or agency responds to your complaint in writing, make sure you keep their response.
