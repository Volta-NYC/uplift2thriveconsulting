We asked some of the most talented folks in the community to talk to us about projects they’ve worked on that they still think about, projects that really meant something. Have you had such an experience? Are you looking for inspiration for your next project? Check out the stories below, they are exciting, entertaining, and most importantly – inspiring.

## Gina Physic![Meet Gina Physic](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779371867954-personal_1779371867856_1779371867856_gina_img_0767.jpeg)

I’m the creator of Forthwrite, a journaling club and a Substack by the same name. The intention behind the journaling club is to use artwork and guided journaling to inspire introspection and cultivate awe. When we experience awe, our tendency to be self-referential goes offline and our sensory tendencies turn on, allowing us to connect with something larger than ourselves. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-gina-physic)

## Adam Land![Meet Adam Land](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779226251274-personal_1779226251204_1779226251204_adam_land_img_3838.png)

One of the most meaningful projects I have worked on is not a single painting, but an ongoing body of work centered on the idea of invisible systems. I am interested in actualities like data signals, infrastructure, and the networks that shape how we experience space and information. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-adam-land-2)

## Belgica Reyes![Meet Belgica Reyes](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779241649489-personal_1779241649397_1779241649397_belgica_reyes_img_4507.jpeg)

One of the most meaningful projects I’ve worked on has been rebuilding my career and identity through AI and digital education after going through a very difficult personal season. For many years, I worked in the world of entrepreneurship, coaching, and digital business creation through my company, Kreacciona. I loved helping women grow their ideas and build businesses around their passions. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-belgica-reyes)

## Allison Dayne![Meet Allison Dayne](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779299808012-personal_1779299807863_1779299807863_allison_dayne_a7r01730jpg.jpg)

Easily A King’s Curtain. At the core, the story explores memory, legacy, performance, and the complicated tenderness that can exist between parent and child. I wanted to create something deeply human that examined what happens when the people we love begin slipping away from us while we’re still trying to hold onto who they once were. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-allison-dayne)

## Xinyuan Zhuan![Meet Xinyuan Zhuan](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779001318731-personal_1779001318605_1779001318605_xinyuan_zhuan_img_8420.jpg)

One of the most meaningful projects I’ve worked on is a conceptual picture book called The Guide to Intracranial Wandering, which I created in 2020. It explores the inner world of the brain and consciousness through a fictional “journey” inside the mind. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-xinyuan-zhuan)

## Gael Palen![Meet Gael Palen](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779209713227-personal_1779209713746_1779209713746_gael_palen_0.jpg)

In my journey as an artist, there have been many meaningful projects I’ve had the privilege to be a part of. From acting in films with themes around mental health, queer love, societal differences and their impacts, to exploring morally gray characters and why they behave the way they do. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-gael-palen)

## Liamel Cohen![Meet Liamel Cohen](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779322481925-personal_1779322483998_1779322483998_liamel_cohen_103_0339.jpg)

My ‘style’ can be considered jarring to most; some even characterize it as gory. To me, they have always brought a sense of overwhelming peace. I had someone important in my life pass away suddenly; we were the same age. They were loved tremendously, a beacon of light and love. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-liamel-cohen)

## Vanessa Frances![Meet Vanessa Frances](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779122692253-personal_1779122692060_1779122692060_vanessa_frances_2x7a9272.png)

The most meaningful project I’ve worked on has been my debut novel, \*Orange Island\*, because it feels like the clearest expression of who I am as a writer and what I care about emotionally, societially, and artistically. The novel came out this spring through Red Rook Press after about three years of work, and it honestly changed me in the process of writing it. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-vanessa-frances)

## Katlego Motaung![Meet Katlego Motaung](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779146768202-personal_1779146767894_1779146767894_katlego_motaung_kat-illustrator.jpg)

This is a tough one. I find that ever a lot of projects that are important to me are often the ones that are self-initiated. Working on personal creative projects, from character art or short comic exercises, gives me an opportunity to work on something I’m passionate about. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-katlego-motaung)

## Gagandeep Singh Gill![Meet Gagandeep Singh Gill](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779154982525-personal_1779154982414_1779154982414_gagandeepsingh_gill_gagan-headshot.jpeg)

I’m the kind of professional that considers every project I’ve made to be just as meaningful, because of the hardships and sacrifices I made to bring those projects to life. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-gagandeep-singh-gill)

## Ariana Hayes![Meet Ariana Hayes](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779223978900-personal_1779223978811_1779223978811_ariana_hayes_ari-3.jpeg)

One of the most meaningful projects I’ve worked on was a campaign centered around diversity and self-expression in fashion. What made it special wasn’t just the brand or the visibility—it was the timing and the story behind it. Early in my modeling journey, I often felt pressure to fit into very specific standards. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-ariana-hayes-2)

## Keege Elliott![Meet Keege Elliott](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779217253854-personal_1779217253632_1779217253632_keege_elliott_sse_2370.jpg)

\*\*What’s been the most meaningful project you’ve worked on?\*\* The most meaningful project I’ve worked on was A Philly Special Christmas, an initiative where three Philadelphia Eagles players release products to raise money for local charities and spread joy during the holiday season. The year I joined, instead of their traditional Christmas albums, we created limited edition plush collectible toys of the players. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-keege-elliott)

## Melvin Pruitt![Meet Melvin Pruitt](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779068856987-personal_1779068856765_1779068856765_melvin_pruitt_1000037905.jpg)

Well to me as far as what I do as far as creating something beautiful for my customers to pick and choose from I treat each sale like new project each time because it is. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-melvin-pruitt)

## Gash The Reaper![Meet Gash The Reaper](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778901342520-personal_1778901341155_1778901341155_gash_thereaper_images.jpg)

Most meaningful project I worked on is Glass Mountain cause I talk about how it feels to work on something you love and not getting nothing in return but the satisfaction that what was said was meant and not a code for help but reason to keep going even when it seems like your done. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-gash-the-reaper)

## Suzanne Whitaker![Meet Suzanne Whitaker](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778989885494-personal_1778989885300_1778989885300_suzanne_whitaker_me-with-bunny-from-monte-vista.jpg)

Gosh, I’ve had quite a few meaningful projects over the many years of art making. One that comes to mind in more recent years is a mural I was asked to design and paint for a domestic violence shelter for women and children. The shelter had some grant money and they’d chosen a building that could be seen from many vantage points. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-suzanne-whitaker)

## Biviana Franco![Meet Biviana Franco](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779151925902-personal_1779151925746_1779151925746_biviana_franco_dsc_5755_1.jpg)

The most meaningful project I have ever worked on is, without a doubt, founding Feel Beautiful Today. Sixteen years ago, I was simply an artist and designer with a deep desire to help people who were facing cancer. At that time, I had close people around me—my aunt and friends—who were going through the difficult journey of cancer. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-biviana-franco-2)

## Bex Wilkinson![Meet Bex Wilkinson](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779028558566-personal_1779028558360_1779028558360_bex_wilkinson_american-cheese-5.jpg)

I just finished a series of paintings this year titled ‘American Cheese.’ I have found myself so very frustrated with the political circus that is happening in the USA under the Trump Administration that I wanted to create a series of paintings that would reflect my angst. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-bex-wilkinson-2)

## Ashley Miah![Meet Ashley Miah](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779227673585-personal_1779227673502_1779227673502_ashley_miah_1c4f14c4-1f35-4fba-a065-b63e906d87da.jpeg)

The most meaningful project I’ve ever worked on has been myself. Not in a self-centered way, but in the sense that I realized my life, identity, and evolution are the foundation of everything I create. For a long time, I poured my creativity into building brands, communities, events, and opportunities for other people. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-ashley-miah-2)

## Amira Gadd![Meet Amira Gadd](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778813445247-personal_1778813444975_1778813444975_amira_gadd_facetune-29-01-2026-19-22-42.jpeg)

One of the last meaningful projects I worked on was the Grammy® nominated album ‘The Colors In My Mind.’ When I started working in the Music Industry in 2018 as a professional songwriter and lyricist, I promised myself I would use my talent to uplift others and have a positive influence in the world. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-amira-gadd)

## Jillian Pfennig![Meet Jillian Pfennig](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778823479588-personal_1778823479196_1778823479196_jillian_pfennig_0780466e-c07a-4012-bc25-450400662f20.jpeg)

The most meaningful project I’ve made came out of the most disorienting period of my life. I had recently suffered a brain injury, and I was in this strange in-between space where the person I’d been was unraveling and the person I was becoming hadn’t shown up yet. I couldn’t talk about it cleanly, so I created the piece instead. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-jillian-pfennig)

## Holly Chester![Meet Holly Chester](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779047812561-personal_1779047812173_1779047812173_holly_chester_holly-chester-final-6588.jpg)

The most meaningful project I’ve worked on is my first poetry book. It’s titled Welcome To My Mind and explores the themes of chaos, self-sabotage, heartbreak, love, and kindness that live within us. This book shares my own journey of self-discovery, as I realized there were many lies I’d believed about myself and the world around me that were holding me back in life. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-holly-chester-2)

## Wendi Zlamal![Meet Wendi Zlamal](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778891564963-personal_1778891564850_1778891564850_wendi_zlamal_e7af6563-a01d-4dac-a9e5-67c1cca0242d.jpg)

Art has been a huge part of me for as long as I can remember, but it wasn’t until I became a mother that I truly discovered my artistic voice. Motherhood has challenged and inspired me in ways I never expected. In many ways, it brought me closer not only to my son, but also to my inner child [. Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-wendi-zlamal)

## Sarah Lorentz![Meet Sarah Lorentz](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778972553978-personal_1778972554621_1778972554621_sarah_lorentz_sarah-lorentz-at-montgomery-botanical-garden-by-josh-aronson-20.jpg)

I just completed a new series for a grant-funded project featuring the Great Rivers Greenway network of trails and connected greenspaces. The greater Saint Louis region has an impressive range of natural environments including: cliffs, bluffs and caves; lakes, creeks and rivers; wetlands, prairies and forests; as well as urban trails. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-sarah-lorentz-2)

## Sophie Spurn![Meet Sophie Spurn](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779058655917-personal_1779058655734_1779058655734_sophie_spurn_sophie-spurn.jpg)

I recently worked with Cirque du Soleil on a high-profile international event. This was such a meaningful job to me because I had wanted to work for Cirque since seeing their shows as a kid. I always thought it was the most spectacular company and group of talented individuals coming together to create magic- and that is truly what my experience was. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-sophie-spurn)

## Nick Zweig![Meet Nick Zweig](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779160386649-personal_1779160386565_1779160386565_nick_zweig_656308636_1669039454111968_2354716940057104752_n.jpg)

My most meaningful project is a short film put out in 2021 called I am Duck. In 2018 had been working for an animation studio, Cartuna, and they were funding small short films as pseudo pilots for shows or just throwing some money at someone with a fun idea. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-nick-zweig)

## Wy’Jerell Lyons![Meet Wy’Jerell Lyons](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778852261261-personal_1778852260668_1778852260668_wyjerell_lyons_img_0479-2.jpeg)

The most meaningful project I’ve worked on is my debut album, My Own Universe: The Prelude to a New World. I’ve been rapping on and off since high school, but music was always something I kept relatively intimate. I never released a full album or seriously pushed my work publicly. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-wyjerell-lyons)

## Josh Wilhelm![Meet Josh Wilhelm](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779100456198-personal_1779100455169_1779100455169_josh_wilhelm_251a0898.jpg)

One of the most meaningful projects I’ve worked on was a cover art piece for my friend Brent Crowe and his song I Never Knew. Brent is a singer-songwriter and producer who believed in me and my ability to create cover art from day one. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-josh-wilhelm-2)

## Eanaj Janae![Meet Eanaj](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779133183999-personal_1779133183868_1779133183868_eanaj_janae_facd1082-1fc8-4e0b-afa5-bb456434e4ce.jpeg)

The most meaningful project I’ve worked on recently is a new piece I created this year centered around archival memory and personal history, using fabrics collected from different chapters of my life. This work marked a shift for me it became less about creating something new and more about honoring what already existed. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-eanaj)

## William Harris![Meet William Harris](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779120493002-personal_1779120492749_1779120492749_william_harris_img_1742.jpg)

one of the most meaningful projects I’ve worked on was helping local businesses and creators completely reshape how they presented themselves online through visual storytelling and branding. What made it meaningful wasn’t just the photography or content creation itself — it was the circumstances around it. A lot of the people I worked with were incredibly talented but overlooked. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-william-harris-3)

## Jihye Kim![Meet Jihye Kim](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778430305367-personal_1778430305283_1778430305283_jihye_kim_img1_jihye.png)

It was especially meaningful to me because I was involved in almost every part of the process, from the initial concept to launching the product to real customers. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-jihye-kim)

## Grace Good![Meet Grace Good](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779296625704-personal_1779296625474_grace-good-modern-circus-performer-nashville.jpeg)

As a modern circus performer based in Nashville, Tennessee, some of the most meaningful projects in my career have involved performing for large live audiences in arenas and major event spaces across the country. There’s something incredibly special about creating a moment where thousands of people collectively stop, react and become completely immersed in a live performance together. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-grace-good-2)

## Shemeka Wright![Meet Shemeka Wright](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779248523878-personal_1779248523115_1779248523115_shemeka_wright_shemeka-wright_sw_078.png)

The most meaningful project I’ve worked on is Glass House, a vertical anthology series I created, produced, directed, and starred in through my company, Wright Positive Films. I’ve spent years doing everything actors are told to do. Training, auditioning, waiting for the right opportunity. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-shemeka-wright-2)

## Carl Cruz![Meet Carl Cruz](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1779135715956-personal_1779135715772_1779135715772_carl_cruz_img_0733.jpeg)

The most meaningful project I’ve ever worked on is La Fotoperrería itself, not as a business, but as a decision. In 2014, after nearly twenty years building a career in photography and photography education, I made a choice that most people around me considered reckless: I walked away from a successful school I had created, Siete Fotógrafos, and committed entirely to one thing: photographing dogs. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-carl-cruz)

## Erykah Townsend![Meet Erykah Townsend](https://canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778976012227-personal_1778976013364_1778976013364_erykah_townsend_img_5130.jpg)

All of my projects are meaningful to me, but a recent one I’ve done is Missed Connections. This project is part of a fellowship I’ve done at Zygote Press in Cleveland, Ohio, alongside fellow artists Jazzee Rozier, Monique Donesia, Briana Robinson, Arfil Pajarillaga, and Kalia Horner, Missed Connections brings together a few different processes within one interactive installation. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-erykah-townsend-2)
