# Reports of investigations and preliminary inquiries

## The Australian Information Commissioner may undertake a Commissioner initiated investigation (CII) into whether the actions of an agency or private sector organisation may be an interference with the privacy of an individual.

Prior to commencing an investigation, the Commissioner may conduct preliminary inquiries, to determine whether to commence a CII.

A CII may look at a specific act or practice, at a systemic problem or recurring pattern in an entity’s practices and processes in handling personal information, or at a practice or problem occurring in more than one entity.

Below are reports of preliminary inquiries and investigations on the Commissioners’ own initiative under Part V of the Privacy Act.

Reports of investigations relating to acts or practices prior to 12 March 2014 use the term ‘own motion investigation’. CIIs that have been opened after 12 March 2014 may result in a formal determination under section 52 of the Privacy Act 1988 (see [Privacy determinations)](https://www.oaic.gov.au/2022/privacy/privacy-assessments-and-decisions/privacy-decisions/privacy-determinations).

CII reports are also published under the [Australian Information Commissioner Case Notes (AICmrCN) series](http://www6.austlii.edu.au/cgi-bin/viewdb/au/cases/cth/AICmrCN/).
