# What is health information?

[Listen](https://app-oc.readspeaker.com/cgi-bin/rsent?customerid=9755&lang=en_au&readclass=page-content&url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.oaic.gov.au%2Fprivacy%2Fyour-privacy-rights%2Fhealth-information%2Fwhat-is-health-information "Listen to this page using ReadSpeaker webReader")

Health information is any [personal information](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/privacy/your-privacy-rights/your-personal-information/what-is-personal-information) about your health or disability. It includes information or opinion about your illness, injury or disability.

Some examples of health information include:

- notes of your symptoms or diagnosis
- information about a health service you’ve had or will receive
- specialist reports and test results
- prescriptions and other pharmaceutical purchases
- dental records
- your genetic information
- your wishes about future health services
- your wishes about potential organ donation
- appointment and billing details
- any other [personal information](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/privacy/your-privacy-rights/your-personal-information/what-is-personal-information) about you when a [health service provider](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/privacy/health-information/what-is-a-health-service-provider) collects it.
