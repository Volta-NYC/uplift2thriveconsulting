Do you remember your first hire? There is so much that goes into building a company, but few things matter as much the quality of the team you assemble. Given the immense importance of team building, we wanted to create a series where we ask entrepreneurs who have been there and done that, to share their stories and lessons with the community.” with “What did you learn as an intern or apprentice?

## Jose Ibarra![Meet Jose Ibarra](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778660828663-personal_1778660828326_1778660828326_jose_ibarra_img_7264.jpeg)

At the beginning, it was just me. I didn’t have a large structure, investors, or a full team behind me — only a vision, strong relationships, and the willingness to make things happen even when resources were limited. Coming from Guatemala and later studying fashion business in Miami, I learned very quickly that in industries like fashion, beauty, and events, relationships are everything. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-jose-ibarra)

## Ismari Nolasco![](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778615468580-1778615468459_ismari_nolasco_dscf5349.jpeg)

I started my TikTok Shop journey by attending creator events and learning everything through experience. As I met more creators, I realized many were struggling with little guidance, difficulty getting samples, and not knowing how to grow or connect with brands. I began helping creator friends by sharing opportunities, teaching them TikTok Shop strategies, and introducing them to events and networking opportunities. [Read More>>](https://canvasrebel.com/meet-ismari-nolasco-2)

## Kathy Kinghorn![Meet Kathy Kinghorn](https://cdn.canvasrebel.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/05/c-1778622168274-personal_1778622168070_1778622168070_kathy_kinghorn_kathykinghorn.png)

We had employees from day one. But it was still very much a scrappy, hands-on start. In the beginning it was me, my business partner, and a front office assistant. We knew our mission from the start. We wanted to build a practice where truly excellent therapists were available, because we believed that when qualified clinicians are accessible, it genuinely changes lives. At the start, it was just me, myself, and I so I did everything from running the business to the actual lawyering and everything in between.
