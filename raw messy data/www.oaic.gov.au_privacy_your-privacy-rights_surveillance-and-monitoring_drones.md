Several laws cover the use of drones in Australia.

If you’ve been photographed or filmed by a drone and the operator is an organisation or agency the _Privacy Act 1988_ covers, then they must comply with the [Australian Privacy Principles](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/privacy/australian-privacy-principles-guidelines). The Privacy Act covers Australian Government agencies and organisations with an annual turnover of more than $3 million, and [some other organisations](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/privacy/the-privacy-act/rights-and-responsibilities#WhoHasResponsibilitiesUnderPrivacyAct). Such an organisation or agency must:

- tell you that your image may be captured before you’re recorded
- make sure recorded [personal information](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/privacy/your-privacy-rights/your-personal-information/what-is-personal-information) is secure and destroyed or de-identified when it is no longer needed

The Privacy Act does not apply to individuals acting in a private capacity. Civil Aviation Safety Authority regulates the use of recreational drones. Visit their [website \- external site](https://www.casa.gov.au/) for a list of rules an individual must follow when operating a drone.

If your neighbour is using a drone and you’re concerned about your privacy, see [Security Cameras](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/privacy/your-privacy-rights/surveillance-and-monitoring/security-cameras#ResidentialSecurityCamera).

If a media or news outlet has used a drone to capture your image without permission, see [Photos and Videos](https://www.oaic.gov.au/_old/privacy/your-privacy-rights/social-media-and-online-privacy/photos).
